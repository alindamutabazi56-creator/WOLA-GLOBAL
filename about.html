Mission & Vision

Mission

To equip young African women with the tools, confidence, and networks needed to grow into positions of influence, while building safe, inclusive communities that nurture leadership, collaboration, and impact.

Vision

A future where African women lead boldly across sectors, brain drain is reversed through purposeful local contribution, and collective talent drives sustainable development for Africa.

1.0 Background

Africa faces the persistent challenge of brain drain, where highly skilled young people leave the continent to pursue opportunities abroad, weakening local capacity for innovation and development. Alongside this is a pressing leadership gap; across sectors, women remain significantly underrepresented in decision-making positions. Cultural, social, and structural barriers often discourage women from pursuing leadership, silencing their voices and narrowing Africa‚Äôs leadership pipeline. This combination of lost talent and underutilised potential creates a vacuum in which the continent‚Äôs progress is slowed. WOLA emerges in response, committed to reversing brain drain and equipping African women with the tools and confidence to lead Africa‚Äôs future.

2.0 Introduction

WOLA is a student-led initiative founded to equip young African women with the tools, confidence, and networks needed to assume positions of influence across sectors, through its tiered structure: social community, self-actualisation, and community development. WOLA fosters leadership from within, ensuring that members not only grow individually but also drive collective progress across Africa.

3.0 Objectives

  • To provide a safe and inclusive environment where African women can share experiences and challenges freely.
  • To build leadership and professional skills through training, mentorship, and practical initiatives.
  • To connect African women across universities and communities, fostering collaboration and unity.
  • To empower members to design and implement projects that address issues affecting African societies.
  • To contribute to reversing brain drain by channelling talent and ideas toward the sustainable development of Africa.

4.0 Target

  • Primary: Young African women studying in universities across the diaspora and Africa.
  • Secondary: Broader African student communities, partner organisations, and allies who support women‚Äôs empowerment and leadership.

5.0 Activities

  • Hosting dialogues and fireside chats on critical issues (e.g. femicide, leadership, mental health, education, economic issues).
  • Leadership and skills workshops tailored to members‚Äô personal and professional growth.
  • Collaborative projects with foundations, NGOs, and community groups addressing African women‚Äôs needs.
  • Establishing inter-university WOLA chapters to expand reach and impact.
  • Mentorship programs connecting members with African women leaders across different sectors.
  • Publishing thought pieces, reports, and creative works through the WOLA Editorial Team.
